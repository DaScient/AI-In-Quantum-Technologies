## Motivation

Cryptography is where the informational nature of quantum mechanics becomes a
security guarantee. **Quantum key distribution** (QKD) lets two parties share a
secret key whose secrecy rests not on computational assumptions but on the laws of
physics: any eavesdropper necessarily disturbs the quantum signals, and that
disturbance is detectable. At the same time, the looming threat of quantum
*attacks* on classical cryptography drives the post-quantum transition. AI sits on
both sides of this contest.

## Theory

In the **BB84** protocol, Alice encodes random bits in randomly chosen bases and
Bob measures in randomly chosen bases; they keep only the rounds where their
bases agree (sifting). Eavesdropping or noise manifests as a **quantum bit error
rate** (QBER). The Shor–Preskill analysis gives the asymptotic secret key rate per
sifted bit as

$$
r = q\left[\,1 - H_2(e_b) - H_2(e_p)\,\right],
$$

where $H_2$ is the binary entropy, $e_b$ and $e_p$ are the bit and phase error
rates, and $q$ is the sifting efficiency. The two entropy terms have clear
meanings: $H_2(e_p)$ is the information potentially leaked to an eavesdropper, and
$H_2(e_b)$ is the cost of reconciling Alice's and Bob's keys via error correction.
When the QBER exceeds about $11\%$ for symmetric channels, the rate falls to zero
and no secret key can be distilled.

Machine learning improves QKD operationally: it optimises and stabilises the
photonic hardware, classifies detector behaviour, and tightens finite-key
parameter estimation from limited data. Conversely, AI accelerates *cryptanalysis*
and side-channel attacks, and the prospect of Shor's algorithm breaking RSA and
elliptic-curve cryptography motivates lattice-based post-quantum schemes whose own
security is increasingly probed with learning-based attacks. Security in the
quantum era is thus a moving target, analysed continually rather than settled
once.

## From theory to code

`aiquantum.crypto` computes the BB84 rate, backing `/api/v1/crypto/key_rate`:

```python
from aiquantum.crypto import bb84_key_rate

print(bb84_key_rate(qber=0.02))   # positive -> secure
print(bb84_key_rate(qber=0.12))   # 0.0      -> above the threshold, insecure
```

## Summary

QKD turns physical law into a secrecy guarantee, with the secret key rate set by
the observed error rate through binary entropies. AI hardens QKD systems and
sharpens both attacks and defences across the post-quantum landscape.
