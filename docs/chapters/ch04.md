## Motivation

Before we can learn from quantum data, we must agree on how a quantum object
becomes data. A state or process is never handed to us as a matrix; it is
inferred from the statistics of measurements. **Tomography** is that inference,
and it is the cleanest illustration of the central tension of the field: complete
characterisation costs resources exponential in system size, so approximation —
often learned — is mandatory.

## Theory

**State tomography.** An $n$-qubit density matrix has $4^n - 1$ real parameters.
Because the tensor-product Pauli operators $\{I, X, Y, Z\}^{\otimes n}$ form an
orthogonal, informationally complete basis, the state can be reconstructed from
their expectation values:

$$
\rho = \frac{1}{2^n} \sum_{P} \langle P \rangle\, P.
$$

Finite sampling makes the raw estimate slightly unphysical; projecting its
eigenvalues onto the probability simplex restores a valid state. This **linear
inversion** is exact in the limit of infinite shots and is the baseline against
which compressed-sensing and neural tomography are measured.

**Process tomography.** A physical operation is a completely positive,
trace-preserving (CPTP) map. Its most convenient classical representation is the
**Pauli transfer matrix** (PTM),

$$
R_{ij} = \frac{1}{2^n}\operatorname{Tr}\!\left[P_i\,\mathcal{E}(P_j)\right],
$$

a real matrix whose columns describe how each input Pauli operator is transformed.
Trace preservation fixes the identity row; complete positivity constrains the
spectrum. The PTM composes by ordinary matrix multiplication, making it ideal for
reasoning about gate sequences and noise.

**The scaling wall.** Both procedures require a number of distinct measurement
settings that grows exponentially in $n$. This is why the textbook's later
chapters turn to neural quantum states, classical shadows, and learned
estimators — each a way to extract the *relevant* information without
reconstructing the entire object.

## Summary

Tomography turns measurements into matrices and exposes the exponential wall that
motivates machine learning. Linear inversion plus physicality projection is the
honest baseline; everything cleverer is judged against it.
