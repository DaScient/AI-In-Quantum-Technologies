# Chapter 23 — Open Questions and Fundamental Limits

> Part VIII: Horizons

## Learning objectives

- Catalogue the major open problems at the AI–quantum interface.
- Distinguish engineering obstacles from fundamental limits.
- Reason carefully about claims of quantum advantage.

## Motivation

A mature field knows what it does not know. This chapter gathers the principal
open questions of quantum AI and, more importantly, teaches the discipline of
separating temporary engineering hurdles from limits imposed by physics or
computational complexity. Intellectual honesty about these boundaries is the mark
of a serious practitioner.

## Theory

**Where is quantum advantage real?** The sharpest open question is the precise
boundary of useful quantum advantage in learning. Dequantisation has dissolved
several proposed speedups (Chapter 16), while learning from quantum data retains
strong theoretical support (Chapter 18). Mapping this boundary rigorously —
characterising the problem structures that admit provable, end-to-end advantage —
is unfinished business.

**Can variational methods scale?** Barren plateaus (Chapter 17) may be a
fundamental obstruction to generic variational learning rather than a tuning
problem. Whether structured ansätze can evade them at scale, for problems we care
about, is unresolved and consequential.

**What are the limits of error correction?** Thresholds, overheads, and the
real-time decoding bottleneck (Chapter 8) determine when fault tolerance becomes
practical. Learned decoders help, but the ultimate trade-offs between code
distance, latency, and physical-qubit budgets are still being charted.

**Fundamental limits.** Some boundaries are permanent: no-cloning forbids copying
unknown states (Chapter 14); the Heisenberg limit caps metrological precision
(Chapter 9); the Holevo bound limits classical information extractable from a
quantum state; and complexity-theoretic beliefs suggest quantum computers do not
crack NP-complete problems efficiently (Chapter 15). Distinguishing these
immovable limits from the movable frontier of engineering — qubit counts,
coherence times, classical simulability — is the central analytical skill this
chapter cultivates. Many bold claims founder precisely on conflating the two.

## From theory to code

The library's transparent baselines are tools for honest assessment: by pairing
every learned method with a classical reference, a reader can measure a claimed
advantage rather than assume it. This methodological stance — quantify, do not
assert — is the practical expression of the chapter's theme.

## Summary

Quantum AI's open questions span the reality of advantage, the scalability of
variational methods, and the limits of error correction, set against permanent
bounds like no-cloning and the Holevo and Heisenberg limits. The essential skill
is telling fundamental limits from engineering frontiers.
